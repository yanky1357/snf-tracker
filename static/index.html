About This Tool

The SNF Ownership Tracker is a public-interest research tool that makes it easy to explore the complex ownership structures of Medicare-enrolled skilled nursing facilities across the United States.

Data Source

All data comes from the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Provider Enrollment, Chain, and Ownership System (PECOS), released under the CMS Open Data Initiative. Dataset reflects ownership as of March 2, 2026 and covers over 14,000 facilities.

How to Read Ownership Chains

Each facility may have multiple owners. When an owner is itself owned by another entity, click View Ownership Chain to trace that relationship upward through as many layers as the data supports.

Owner Type Definitions

  • Private Equity — A private equity company holds a direct or indirect ownership interest.
  • Holding Company — An entity created primarily to hold ownership interests in other companies.
  • Investment Firm — A firm that invests capital in exchange for ownership stakes.
  • LLC — A limited liability company holds an ownership interest.
  • Corporation — A corporate entity holds an ownership interest.
  • REIT — A real estate investment trust has an ownership or financial interest.
  • Non-Profit — The facility is owned or operated by a non-profit organization.
  • Trust/Trustee — Ownership is held in trust.

Limitations

  • Ownership chains may be incomplete if a holding company is not itself enrolled in PECOS.
  • Facility states are derived from owner address data when direct facility addresses are unavailable.
  • Data reflects enrollment records and may differ from current operational ownership.

Data Currency

Dataset: CMS SNF All Owners, released 2026-03-02. This tool is not affiliated with CMS.
